motivation: |-
  Text-to-logic pipelines stall at one crux: when strict symbolic unification fails, an LLM must fuzzy-match predicates and supply background knowledge, and that interface is exactly where hallucination re-enters and silently corrupts every downstream deduction. The dangerous hallucinations are not random nonsense but PLAUSIBLE, high-confidence false facts. Practitioners have no quantitative, label-free knob there: self-consistency and LLM-as-judge are heuristic; ontology-constraint filtering needs rich trusted constraints and catches only encoded violations; and the strongest uncertainty-quantification methods (conformal factuality, conformal selection + BH, multiple-testing hallucination detection, coherent factuality, and the newest online and differentiable variants) all need a LABELED calibration set and certify the final answer or claim set, not the neural-to-symbolic admission boundary.

  Genomics and proteomics solved an isomorphic label-poor problem -- selecting true signals among overwhelming noise with a guaranteed FDR and no ground truth -- via the knockoff filter and target-decoy competition, and learned the two ways the trick breaks: decoys 'too unrealistic to fool' the scorer make FDR optimistic (cured by property-matched decoys), and entrapment FDP must be estimated with a valid upper bound built INDEPENDENTLY of the decoys. We import matched decoys, the valid combined estimator, and construction-independence to the fuzzy-unification boundary, turning 'reduce hallucination' from a best-effort aspiration into a self-corroborated, label-free, auditable quantity with per-leaf certificates.

  The two-anchor design makes the value claim honest. A label-free FDR knob is worth building only if it is BOTH calibrated AND useful where plausible hallucinations actually bite. CLUTRR's closed kinship vocabulary is the right place to prove CALIBRATION (free crisp gold, exact realized FDR) but the wrong place to prove USEFULNESS (the relation set is tiny and well-known). Re-DocRED -- realistic Wikipedia prose, 96 open relation types, document-level multi-hop, human gold with evidence spans -- is the right place to prove operational usefulness, with a fixed shared extraction schema so the neuro-symbolic pipeline, a plain zero-label confidence threshold, CoT, RAG, and labeled conformal are all measured on the same triples at matched recall. The plain confidence threshold is the load-bearing comparator: it shows whether the decoy-competition machinery adds value over naive thresholding at the SAME zero-label budget, so the wedge cannot collapse to 'a threshold was enough.'

  Why it matters even at coarse alpha: NO existing label-free method offers ANY FDR knob at the fuzzy-unification interface. A first, auditable, zero-operation-label control -- even at alpha ~0.1 -- converts an uncontrolled hallucination channel into a tunable one. Because LLM decoys carry no theoretical guarantee, the most probable outcome is partial control; the Re-DocRED document-level predictive account (S5) converts a likely-partial result into a generalizable mechanism-level insight about WHEN the calibration holds.

Resample at the **document** level, not the candidate level.\\n\\n### C.2 Procedure (concrete)\\n\\n```\\nfor b in 1..B:\\n    docs_b   = sample WITH REPLACEMENT from {documents}      # block = whole document, preserves within-doc dependence\\n    corpus_b = concat(all candidate records of docs in docs_b)\\n    t_b, S_b, fdr_b = knockoff_plus_threshold(W of corpus_b, alpha)   # re-run the WHOLE gate on the resample\\n    ent_b   = entrapment_fdp(N_T(corpus_b), N_E(corpus_b), r, estimator)   # and the entrapment bound\\n    stat_b  = realized_FDP_statistic(corpus_b)               # whatever statistic the disconfirmation tests\\ncollect {stat_b}; report percentile CI (2.5%, 97.5%).\\n# Primary disconfirmation: does the CI lie ENTIRELY on the anti-conservative (above-alpha) side?\\n```\\n\\n- **Recommended `B ≥ 2000`** (percentile-CI tail stability; `B=2000` gives stable 2.5/97.5 quantiles).\\n- **Statistic of interest** = realized FDP vs. the operative `α`; the CI used by the **primary\\n  disconfirmation** is this document-block percentile CI.\\n\\n### C.3 Two granularities — do not conflate\\n\\n- **FDP bootstrap** (above): resample **documents with replacement**, recompute the gate, get an FDP/FDR CI.\\n- **Cross-validation for S5** (leave-one-cluster-out / leave-one-document-out): a **separate** generalization\\n  check — partition documents, hold one out, evaluate. It is **not** the FDP CI and must not be mixed into the\\n  bootstrap loop.\\n\\n### C.4 Pseudo-procedure — `doc_block_bootstrap`\\n\\n```\\nfunction doc_block_bootstrap(per_doc_records, statistic_fn, B=2000):\\n    stats = []\\n    D = number of documents\\n    for b in 1..B:\\n        idx   = sample D document-indices WITH replacement\\n        boot  = concat(per_doc_records[i] for i in idx)\\n        stats.append( statistic_fn(boot) )\\n    point = statistic_fn(per_doc_records)                    # plug-in point estimate on full data\\n    return (point, percentile(stats, 2.5), percentile(stats, 97.5))\\n```\\n\\n---\\n\\n## D. Null sign-flip property + two LLM failure modes + isolated-vs-batched discriminator\\n\\n### D.1 The sole validity condition (verbatim)\\n\\nBarber & Candès, **Lemma 1 (i.i.d. signs for the nulls)** [1]:\\n> *\\\"Let ε ∈ {±1}ᵖ be a sign sequence independent of W, with ε_j = +1 for all nonnull j and ε_j ~iid {±1} for\\n> null j. Then (W₁,…,W_p) =d (W₁·ε₁,…,W_p·ε_p).\\\"*\\n\\ni.e. **for genuinely-false (null) candidates the sign of `W_i` is an independent fair coin (P=0.5), independent\\nof `|W_i|` and of the signs of the true candidates** [1]. Consequence (eq. 1.10) [1]:\\n`#{j: β_j=0 and W_j ≥ t} =d #{j: β_j=0 and W_j ≤ −t}`, which is what makes `#{W_i ≤ −t}` a valid estimate of the\\nfalse admissions above `t`. The **TDC \\\"equal-chance\\\" assumption** [5] is the same statement in competition\\nlanguage: *a false real candidate and its matched decoy are exchangeable in the high-score tail* (each equally\\nlikely to win).\\n\\n> **Honesty caveat (state in the paper).** For LLM decoys there is **no construction-level proof** of Lemma 1\\n> (unlike Gaussian knockoffs). The gate is therefore an **empirically-validated calibration**, not a theorem;\\n> D.4 diagnostics must show the fair-coin property *holds in the tail* on labeled data.\\n\\n### D.2 Two anti-conservative failure channels (reproduce this table)\\n\\n| # | Failure channel | Mechanism | Effect on FDP | Mitigation |\\n|---|-----------------|-----------|---------------|------------|\\n| 1 | **Within-document score correlation** | Null `W` signs dependent within a document → variance/floor understated | Floor/CI too tight (anti-conservative) | **Document-block bootstrap** (Sub-task C) |\\n| 2 | **Batched-scoring contrast effects** | Model implicitly detects the fabricated/odd item among co-presented candidates and depresses decoy scores → decoys lose \\\"too often\\\" | FDP **optimistically** low (anti-conservative) | **ISOLATED, provenance-blinded, order-randomized** scoring as default |\\n\\n### D.3 Isolated-vs-batched discriminator (pre-registered test)\\n\\n```\\nRun a LABELED slice under BOTH scoring regimes:\\n  - ISOLATED : each candidate scored in its own call, provenance hidden, order randomized\\n  - BATCHED  : candidates co-presented in one call\\nDecision:\\n  if ISOLATED restores the FDR-vs-alpha diagonal (calibrated) but BATCHED is anti-conservative\\n        -> the anti-conservatism was a SCORING ARTIFACT (contrast effect); isolated scoring fixes it.\\n  if anti-conservatism PERSISTS under ISOLATED\\n        -> genuine decoy NON-exchangeability (decoys are \\\"too easy\\\"); fix the decoy family (Sub-task E).\\n```\\n**Default = isolated scoring for 100% of the confirmatory set.** Headroom is bought by **prompt caching of the\\ndocument prefix**, *not* by batching (batching reintroduces channel #2).\\n\\n### D.4 Tail diagnostics (measurement only — NEVER consumed by the gate)\\n\\n1. **Tail-conditioned win-rate** of matched (real-false, decoy) pairs **above the admission cutoff `t`** should\\n   be ≈ 0.5 (the fair-coin check of D.1).\\n2. **Upper-tail two-sample CDF test** on real-false vs. decoy scores restricted to the admission region\\n   (`scores ≥ t`): recommended **one-sided two-sample Kolmogorov–Smirnov** (or a tail-restricted Mann–Whitney\\n   U) testing whether decoy scores are stochastically *smaller* than real-false scores in the tail (which would\\n   signal non-exchangeability). \\\"Tail\\\" is delimited by `scores ≥ operative knockoff+ cutoff t`.\\n\\n---\\n\\n## E. Plausibility / property-matched decoy generation\\n\\n### E.1 The load-bearing principle (DeepCoy, verbatim cautionary evidence)\\n\\nDeepCoy [9] generates **property-matched** decoys: decoys must **reproduce the property/score distribution of\\nthe true positives** and must **not be \\\"too easy.\\\"** Too-easy/biased decoys let the scorer separate on\\nartifacts and make the estimated FDR **optimistic**. Quantified by **DOE (Deviation from Optimal Embedding):**\\nDeepCoy improves DOE by an average of **81% (0.166 → 0.032 on DUD-E)** and **66% (0.109 → 0.038 on DEKOIS\\n2.0)** while \\\"introducing no discernible additional risk of false negatives\\\" [9].\\n\\n> **Text translation.** An LLM decoy must reproduce the **score distribution of genuine plausible\\n> hallucinations** — same entity types, surface form, specificity — so the *only* feature distinguishing it\\n> from a real-but-false fact is document non-entailment.\\n\\n### E.2 Primary decoy family — document-conditioned counterfactuals (+ negative control)\\n\\n- **PRIMARY: document-conditioned counterfactual decoys** — maximally-plausible, document-**non-entailed**\\n  facts/bridges drawn from the model's own prior **conditioned on the document**. Generation recipe (prompt\\n  sketch): *\\\"Given DOCUMENT and the relation/entity-type schema R(·,·), produce a fact `R(a,b)` that is\\n  plausible for this genre and these entities but **is NOT stated or entailed by the document**; match the\\n  surface form and specificity of the document's real facts.\\\"* Predicted tail-win-rate ≈ 0.5.\\n- **NEGATIVE CONTROL (keep, expect anti-conservative): random type-matched swaps** — replace an argument with a\\n  random type-matched entity. Deliberately retained as the baseline that *should* read anti-conservative\\n  (decoys too easy → optimistic FDR), validating the diagnostic.\\n\\n### E.3 Non-entailment verification gate (every decoy must pass)\\n\\nAn **accidentally-entailed** decoy is actually true → would bias the FDR **conservatively**. So every decoy\\npasses an **independent entailment check** against the document: NLI-style, or a **separate isolated LLM call**\\nasking *\\\"Is this statement entailed by the text? yes/no.\\\"* Reject (regenerate) if entailed. **Report the\\ncontamination rate** and run a **contamination sensitivity analysis** (sweep the rejection threshold).\\n*\\\"Plausibility-matched\\\"* is defined as: decoy and real share entity types / surface form / specificity, so the\\nonly difference is entailment.\\n\\n### E.4 Pseudo-procedures\\n\\n```\\nfunction generate_decoy(document, candidate_real):\\n    schema = relation_and_type_schema(candidate_real)\\n    decoy  = LLM(\\\"plausible, type/surface/specificity-matched fact under {schema} \\\"\\n                 \\\"that is NOT entailed by:\\\\n{document}\\\")\\n    return decoy\\n\\nfunction verify_non_entailment(document, decoy):           # independent, isolated call (or NLI model)\\n    return LLM_isolated(\\\"Is '{decoy}' entailed by the text? Answer yes/no.\\\\n{document}\\\") == \\\"no\\\"\\n# Accept decoy only if verify_non_entailment is True; log contamination_rate = #entailed / #generated.\\n```\\n\\n---\\n\\n## F. Upper-tail label-free score elicitation — ranked shortlist\\n\\nGoal: a per-candidate score whose **upper tail** discriminates true from false (the admission region). Tail\\ncalibration matters far more than average calibration here.\\n\\n### F.1 Candidates with reported tail behavior\\n\\n1. **Verbalized confidence** (model states 0–100/0–1). **Overconfident in the upper tail**: Xiong et al.\\n   (ICLR 2024) find LLMs are *overconfident* when verbalizing, \\\"potentially imitating human patterns\\\";\\n   calibration improves with scale but remains far from ideal; human-inspired prompting mitigates with\\n   diminishing returns in advanced models [10]. → use as a **floor baseline**.\\n2. **Token-logprob-derived score** (sequence/avg token logprob, or yes/no-token probability of an\\n   entailment-framed prompt).
